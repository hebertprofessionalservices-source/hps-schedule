Your Lead Pipeline

1. Identified
Found on LinkedIn, Google Maps, or referral. Not contacted yet.
2. Contacted
Sent connection request, cold email, or DM. Awaiting reply.
3. Engaged
They responded! Now in conversation — ask the key question: "What's the most frustrating part of it right now?"
4. Call Booked
Discovery call scheduled. Prep your questions and talking points. Send a Calendly link.
5. Proposal Sent
They're interested. Service Agreement or scope sent. Follow up in 48 hours no matter what.
6. CLOSED 🎉
Signed, deposited, work begins. Ask for a testimonial after delivery. Ask for a referral.

→ Emails sent → replies received
→ LinkedIn connections sent → accepted
→ DMs sent → responses
→ Discovery calls taken → proposals sent
→ Proposals sent → closed
→ Revenue this week vs. last week

Revenue-Building Pro Tips

Strategies that actually move the needle for solo service businesses.

🎯
Warm Before Cold
Comment on a prospect's LinkedIn post BEFORE sending them a DM or email. They'll recognize your name — and your message won't feel cold anymore.
📊
Track Everything
Use a simple spreadsheet: Name, Company, Contact Method, Date, Status, Next Step. Review it every Friday. What you track, you improve.
💬
Lead With Pain
Never open with "I offer bookkeeping services." Open with "Most small businesses lose hours every week to manual reporting — does that sound familiar?" Make them feel seen first.
🔗
Referral Partners Are Gold
One CPA partnership can be worth 10 clients. Find CPAs who don't offer bookkeeping cleanup and ask to be their go-to person. This is your fastest path to consistent leads.
📈
Offer a Free Diagnostic
Offer to do a free 30-minute financial 'health check' call. No pitch — just pure value. Show them one problem you found. The close happens naturally when they trust you.
⏰
The 48-Hour Rule
If someone doesn't reply, follow up at exactly 48 hours. One sentence: "Wanted to make sure this didn't get buried!" — Most closes happen on the 2nd or 3rd follow-up.

Free Tools to Use Right Now

Hunter.io (Free)
Find email addresses for local business owners from their website domain. 25 free lookups/month — enough to get started.
Mailtrack (Free)
See when your cold emails are opened. Know exactly when to follow up for maximum response rate.
Canva (Free)
Create professional LinkedIn post graphics, carousels, and infographics in minutes. No design skills needed.
Loom (Free)
Record short video walkthroughs to send to prospects instead of long emails. Personal, memorable, and way harder to ignore.
Calendly (Free)
Give prospects a link to book discovery calls — removes all the back-and-forth scheduling. Looks professional instantly.
Google Sheets (Free)
Your lead tracker. Columns: Name, Company, Source, Date Contacted, Status, Notes, Next Step. Simple and powerful.
LinkedIn (Free)
Post 5x/week. Comment daily. Connect 10–15 people/day. This is your #1 free marketing channel — treat it like a job.

Revenue Comes From Relationships
You don't need 100 clients. You need 3–5 great ones to start. Focus every single day on conversations, not just connections. Every person you talk to is a future client or a referral source. Showing up consistently is your superpower.
